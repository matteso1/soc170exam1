# Lecture 1.3: How do we know what we know about populations? (1/27)

This lecture will discuss the types of data that are used to study populations and current issues related to demographic data. In particular, we will focus on the US Census 2020. We then move beyond censuses to think about other sources of population data, such as statistics about fertility, mortality, and population growth.

## Readings

- Sullivan, Theresa. (2020) "Coming to our census: How social statistics underpin our democracy." *Harvard Data Science Review*. **Focus on sections 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, and 10**
- [optional] How the US Census misses people of color - and why it is so harmful. *The Guardian*, February 2020.
- [optional] Giattino et al. (2021). "Excess mortality during the Coronavirus pandemic (COVID-19)." https://ourworldindata.org/excess-mortality-covid
