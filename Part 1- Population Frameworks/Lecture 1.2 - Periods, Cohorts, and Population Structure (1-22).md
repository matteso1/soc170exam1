# Lecture 1.2: Periods, Cohorts, and Population Structure (1/22)

What can we learn about a population by looking at its age distribution? Demographers often say that a population can't forget its past; this is because past patterns of fertility, mortality, and migration are indelibly imprinted in the age structure. Today's readings and lecture will cover how to interpret a population pyramid and begin to discuss how the age structure and other population characteristics influence our social organization.
