The role of Demand and Diffusion Theories

Causes of Fertility Decline:
Demand and Diffusion Theories
• Demand theories – as the “cost” of children increases, 
demand for contraception will increase
• Investment in children costs $$, easier to do for fewer kids
• Cost!
• Diffusion theories – ideas about the value of children and the 
acceptability of smaller families spread through social 
networks, education, and the media
• Norms!

Coercive vs Rights-based 
Family Planning Policies
• Coercive – 
• Policy, practices, or limitations applied to a population by a 
government so that people do not have full agency or control over 
their fertility choices
• E.g. forced sterilization in India + US
• Could be Pro-natalist, or anti-natalist

---
Coercive vs Rights-based 
Family Planning Policies
• Coercive – 
• Policy, practices, or limitations applied to a population by a 
government so that people do not have full agency or control over 
their fertility choices
• Could be Pro-natalist, or anti-natalist
• Rights-based – 
• put primacy on human rights 
• people’s ability to make informed choices about how many children 
they want, and to achieve those fertility goals
• Not only allow people to limit their fertility, but empower people to 
have as many children as they desire

Use, Demand, and Unmet Need for 
Contraceptives
• Contraceptive use and demand vary across populations
• The difference between demand and use can be defined as “unmet 
need” 
• Unmet need varies around the world, and the reasons for need being 
“unmet” vary too
• Maybe better to think of unmet need as unmet for a specific reason
• In the US, access to effective contraception may still be cost-constrained
• But reasons are very individual!
