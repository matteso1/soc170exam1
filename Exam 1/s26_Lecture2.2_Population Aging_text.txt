• Biological Age
• Combines information from multiple physiological systems to estimate 
individual’s positions on aging trajectory
• Calculated from biomarkers – measures of metabolic, cardiovascular, 
inflammatory, kidney, liver, and lung functioning
• Related to mortality risk, biological functioning
• Influenced by environment (e.g. pollution), behavior (e.g. smoking), genetics, 
random variation

“Healthy” Life Expectancy
• Expected years of life lived in good health
• Often based on measures of functional limitation that are 
sufficient to impair daily activities:
• E.g. standing, getting dressed, preparing food

---
Aging vs Longevity Effects on prevalence of 
health issues
• “Aging effect” = increases in morbidity + mortality caused by increased 
median chronological age of a population
• As people are older, health issues become more common
• Mortality increases, but not for bad reasons!
• Longevity effect = Decreased median biological age of a population 
improves health
• Population health is improving! Mortality is declining!
• “75 is the new 65”
• Reduces prevalence of health issues (all else equal)

Population Aging: Potential Costs
• Economic
•  unfunded support systems for the elderly
• Or, increased funding to aged means less funding elsewhere.
•  economic burden on young workers (taxes, caregiving, etc.)
•  slowing economic growth
• Non-economic
•  less innovation, more risk-aversion
•  weaker security relative to younger nations
