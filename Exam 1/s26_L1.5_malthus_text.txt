Why do populations continue to grow when 
the fertility rate declines?
• Population momentum
• Population growth continues even after the birth rates fall
• When a large proportion of women are in the childbearing 
years, the total number of births can increase even when the 
rate of childbearing per woman falls
• Time lag – momentum keeps populations growing for many 
years after fertility declines
• Implication – fertility changes now don’t have big effects for a 
long time.
