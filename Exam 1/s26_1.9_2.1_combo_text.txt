advocate to their government on making safe abortion available, providing legal 
abortion services, or provide advice on where to get an abortion

Life Span vs. Life Expectancy
• Life span = length of an individual life
• Life expectancy = average length of life in a population
• (average of entire population’s lifespans)
• e0 = life expectancy at age 0 (birth) 
• e25 = life expectancy at age 25
• …

What is life expectancy?
• Average length of life in a population at a given time
• Also: the average age at death at a given time

---
Calculating Period Life Expectancy (e0)
• The average number of years a baby born today would live if 
today’s birth cohort experienced today’s age-specific and 
sex-specific mortality rates as they move through life 
• Like the total fertility rate, this is a period rate estimated for a 
synthetic cohort

Problems with Life Expectancy: 
Period vs. Cohort measures again! 
• Like period measures of fertility (TFR), Life Expectancy can only 
take a cross-sectional snapshot
• Does not actually tell you how long you might expect to live!
• Also fluctuates a lot! (like TFR – remember covid!)

• Average length of life (age at death) in a birth cohort

Measuring “Healthy” Life Expectancy
• Expected years of life lived in good health
• Often based on measures of functional limitation that are 
sufficient to impair daily activities:
• E.g. standing, getting dressed, preparing food
• Morbidity = prevalence of disease in a population
• Could be measured as: physical dysregulation, disease 
onset, functional limitations
