Population Pyramids: Past, Present, and 
Future
•Past – preserves past events in the age structure
•e.g. what impact did past events have on populations?
•Present – allows us to easily see who is at risk of 
demographic events in the immediate future. 
•e.g. what percent of the population is at high risk of mortality?
•Future – permits inferences about future outlook of a 
population (2 parts – growth/decline + dependency ratios)
•e.g. how many people will need Medicare in 20 years?
