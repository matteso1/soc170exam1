# Lecture 4.2: Race and Immigration (4/9)

Race and racial ideologies have always informed immigration policy. Today takes a look at the history of immigration policy in the US, and how race has always been a part of US perspectives on immigration. We will also examine racialization, or how racial groups are constructed through social and political processes.

## Readings

- Brown, Hana E., Jennifer A. Jones, and Andrea Becker. 2018. "The Racialization of Latino Immigrants in New Destinations: Criminality, Ascription, and Countermobilization." *RSF: The Russell Sage Foundation Journal of the Social Sciences* 4(5): 118-40. DOI: 10.7758/RSF.2018.4.5.06.
