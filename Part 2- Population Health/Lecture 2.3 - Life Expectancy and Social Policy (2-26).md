# Lecture 2.3: Life Expectancy and Social Policy (2/26)

Since 2000, the mid-life mortality rates have been increasing in the United States, which caused life expectancy to decrease in 2016 and 2017. Although life expectancy rose in 2018 and 2019, the COVID-19 pandemic has reversed these gains. What causes health reversals? Have recent health reversals in the United States been evenly distributed across the populations, or have some sub-groups been more affected than others? Why is health in the US so much worse than in other rich countries?

## Readings
