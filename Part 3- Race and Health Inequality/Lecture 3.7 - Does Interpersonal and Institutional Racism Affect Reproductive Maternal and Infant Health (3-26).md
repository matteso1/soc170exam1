# Lecture 3.7: Does interpersonal and institutional racism affect reproductive, maternal, and infant health? (3/26)

Who is able to have the number of children that they want? As many as one-quarter of births in the U.S. are unintended, either mistimed or unwanted. At the same time, many women and their partners struggle to achieve the level of childbearing that they want. Today's readings and lecture dive into these topics, considering both how we measure these issues and how reproductive justice provides a theoretical frame for understanding who is most likely to achieve their fertility goals.

## Readings

- Roberts, Dorothy (2015) "Reproductive Justice, Not Just Rights." *Dissent*. 62(4): 79-82
- Claire Cain Miller. (2023) Childbirth Is Deadlier for Black Families Even When They're Rich, Expansive Study Finds. *New York Times*. February 12, 2023
- Something extra, for those interested in the effect of the pandemic on racial inequalities in maternal mortality: Maternal Deaths Rose During the First Year of the Pandemic. *New York Times*
